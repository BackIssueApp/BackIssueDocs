# Gamify

The **Gamify plugin** turns reading into a quest. Earn XP for every page and
issue, level up through comic-flavored titles, keep a daily streak alive, chase
achievements, and clear a weekly quest board generated from **your own
library**. Households get a monthly co-op quest and a leaderboard.

Reading history comes from the **[Comic Reader](reading)** plugin — install
that too (it's what records what you read).

## Playing

**Quests** in the sidebar opens the board:

- **Level & XP** — issues and pages read become XP; levels carry titles from
  *Newsstand Browser* up to *Legend*. Level-ups fire a celebration and top up
  your streak-freeze bank.
- **Streaks** — read every day to build a streak. **Freeze tokens** (earned at
  streak milestones and level-ups) auto-cover a missed day.
- **Yearly goal** — set an issues-per-year target and watch the bar fill.
- **Weekly quests** — up to four auto-generated missions: finish a series
  you're close on, complete a reading list, clear your oldest backlog issues,
  or keep a reading pace. Three rerolls a week.
- **Achievements** — a medal wall for milestones (first issue, full series,
  five decades, marathon days, iron streaks…).
- **Household** — a monthly co-op quest everyone contributes to, plus a
  per-month leaderboard. Any user can hide themselves from both with the
  opt-out on the Household tab.

Your profile page shows the level card at a glance, and completions, unlocks
and level-ups land in the notification bell (and any
[Notifications Hub](notifications) channels).

## Fair play

Only reading done **in the reader** scores — "mark read" updates your library
but earns nothing, re-reads never double-count, and configurable daily caps
bound what a single day can earn toward XP, quests and the leaderboard. Streak
days need a few real pages, so a one-page tap doesn't keep one alive.

## Settings

**Settings → Plugins → Gamify**: master switch, household features on/off,
notifications on/off, the level-up celebration, weekly quest count, and the
fair-play caps (issues/day, pages/day, minimum streak pages).
